Ancestral Homeland

Mardin & Mesopotamia

Mardin is more than a city — it is the cradle of our family's identity. Perched on a hillside overlooking the vast Mesopotamian plain, this ancient city has been home to civilizations for millennia and remains the spiritual heart of the Hısrım legacy.

The Land

5,000 Years of Civilization

The volcanic soils and limestone foundations of this region have nurtured vineyards for thousands of years. The unique microclimate of southeastern Anatolia creates conditions found nowhere else — producing grapes of unparalleled character.

5000 Years of History
Cultural Heritage

Deyrülzafaran Monastery

The Deyrülzafaran Monastery, one of the oldest active monasteries in the world, stands as a testament to the region's spiritual and cultural depth. The Hısrım family maintains a deep connection to this sacred tradition.

Identity

A Crossroads of Cultures

Mardin stands at the intersection of civilizations — Syriac, Kurdish, Arab, and Turkish traditions have coexisted here for centuries. This cultural richness is woven into the very fabric of the Hısrım family identity.

A family heritage spanning 325 years. From Mardin to the world.
