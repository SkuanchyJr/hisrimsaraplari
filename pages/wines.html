The Wines

Tradition in Every Bottle

Hısrım wine is not a commercial product — it is a continuation of family tradition. Born from ancient vines in the volcanic soils of Mesopotamia, each bottle carries the weight of centuries of knowledge, care, and devotion.

The Wine

Dry Red — Limited Production

Crafted from meticulously selected Mardin grapes, aged in French oak barrels specially imported for this purpose. A wine of noble character, long life, and uncompromising quality.

  • Origin Mardin, Mesopotamia
  • Vines Ancient (100–200 years)
  • Soil Volcanic & Limestone
  • Aging 10 months in French oak
  • Potential Age-worthy (up to 20 years)
The Terroir

Where Earth Becomes Wine

The volcanic soils and white limestone of Mardin create extraordinary conditions for viticulture. Red earth rich in minerals, a climate sculpted over millennia, and ancient vines whose roots reach deep into history — together they produce grapes of unparalleled character. The wine born from this terroir can age gracefully for up to 20 years.

5,000 Years of Viticulture
200 Year-Old Vines
20 Years Aging Potential
The Mahzen

10 Months in French Oak

Deep in the cellars of Mardin, time works its quiet alchemy. Our Kırmızı Sek — Dry Red Wine — rests for 10 months in oak barrels specially imported from France. Here, in the darkness and silence, the wine develops the complex aromas and velvety character that define Hısrım.

Limited production. Each bottle is a testament to patience, tradition, and the pursuit of the highest quality.

Cultural Purpose

More Than Wine

This is more than winemaking — it is a cultural responsibility. Part of the proceeds from Hısrım wine support the Deyrülzafaran Monastery, preserving Syriac Orthodox traditions and the artisanal heritage of the region.

A family heritage spanning 325 years. From Mardin to the world.
